Tactopus,
Inclusive Ed-tech

A play-based, early learning startup I founded during design school in response to accessibility issues I discovered during a research project. Tactopus makes playful mixed-reality learning aids designed for children with disabilities.

Popcore: Gaming Products

Popcore is a studio based in Berlin, that makes beautiful mobile games designed to be addictive. As part of the R&D team, I conceptualised and prototyped (on Framer) multiple product explorations: experimental approaches to hyper-casual game discovery, streaming platform for mobile games and a social platform for HTML mini games.

One part of the product design work I particularly enjoyed, was connecting with a community of college students to run an experiment on user generated, memefied game content.

FeelXR

Product design for a Canadian augmented reality startup working on beinging the power of AR to large office furniture labels. FeelXR enables furniture companies to make their products vieable with AR by just adding a line of code to their own product pages. The web experience includes ways to upload data about the furniture, and a dashboard to monitor products' performance live.

accessibility, HCI, gesture interactions

Edge: Accessible Keyboard

Design of a soft-keyboard that uses the edges of the phone screen as tangible marker, so users with vision loss can type faster. The edges become the reference framework for the fingers, and text input is by swiping the letters in from the edges.

This project was a submission to CHI Student Design Competition 2016 and was judged one of the top 4 to present at the conference in San Jose.
